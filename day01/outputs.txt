Q1: Prompt engineering is the strategic art and science of designing and refining inputs, known as prompts, to effectively communicate with and guide artificial intelligence models, particularly large language models (LLMs). Its core purpose is to elicit precise, relevant, and high-quality outputs from these AIs for specific tasks.

Instead of just typing a question, prompt engineers craft detailed instructions, provide context, offer examples (few-shot learning), define desired formats, or even assign a 'persona' to the AI. This iterative process involves testing, analyzing outputs, and adjusting prompts to improve accuracy, reduce hallucinations, and ensure the AI's response aligns perfectly with the user's intent. Ultimately, it’s about unlocking the full potential of AI by giving it optimal directions.
Q2: Prompt engineering is the art and science of designing and refining inputs (prompts) to effectively steer AI models toward producing precise, desired outputs. It's crucial for optimizing AI performance and ensuring relevance.

**Steps typically involve:**
1.  **Understand AI:** Know its capabilities and limitations.
2.  **Define Goal:** Clearly articulate the task and expected outcome.
3.  **Draft Initial Prompt:** Craft a preliminary input.
4.  **Iterate & Refine:** Test, analyze outputs, and systematically adjust the prompt until optimal results are achieved.

**Requirements for an excellent prompt:**
*   **Clarity:** Use simple, unambiguous language.
*   **Specificity:** Provide precise instructions regarding the task, format, and tone.
*   **Context:** Include all necessary background information.
*   **Constraints:** Define boundaries (e.g., word count, persona).
*   **Examples (Few-shot):** Offer illustrative input-output pairs to demonstrate the desired pattern.

*   **Clarity:** Unambiguous language; easy for the AI to understand.
*   **Specificity:** Explicitly state what to do and what to avoid.
*   **Conciseness:** Avoid unnecessary words while retaining detail.
*   **Context:** Provide relevant background information or roles.
*   **Constraints:** Define output length, format, tone, or style.

Comparison, Analysis, and Conclusion
After running the code and reviewing the outputs, it is highly evident that providing clear instructions, details, rules, and constraints leads to significantly better results. Looking at the sequential progression from Prompt 1 to Prompt 5, each level clearly increased the model's accuracy and specificity compared to the previous one:

Prompt 1 (Basic): The output was very general and vague. This happened because I only asked a basic question without providing any specific details, context, or guidelines.

Prompt 2 (Improved): The response became much clearer and more specific. By adding extra details and asking for the exact steps and requirements to write an excellent prompt, the model gave a more structured answer.

Prompt 3 (Detailed): The output improved even further. By sharing context about myself (as a summer intern) and assigning a clear role to the model, it tailored the explanation perfectly to my learning level.

Prompt 4 (Creative): The output achieved an even higher level of quality and engagement. I instructed the model to act as an experienced prompt engineer using a creative, organized, and sequential approach, which made the technical concepts much more relatable.

Prompt 5 (Constraints): This prompt yielded the absolute best and most practical result. I assigned an expert role, defined a strict structure, and set clear constraints (asking for direct bullet points and eliminating conversational filler). The model followed every rule perfectly and delivered a high-density, actionable response.

Conclusion:
Ultimately, this experiment clearly proves that the more tailored, structured, and clear your prompt is, the more accurate and high-quality the final result will be.
